The purpose of the project is to answer the research question: “Is there a significant difference in the mean time taken to obtain the 3 star hotel list between the two websites?”

The data collection phase involved each participant recording the time taken to display the list of 3 star hotels for each location. The website to be searched first was random for each participant. A paired t-test of the gathered data was then carried out to determine the statistical significance between the two websites.

A null and alternative hypothesis was determined. The test statistic then had to be calculated by finding the sample size, degrees of freedom, sum of the differences, sum of the squared differences, standard deviation of the differences and finally the test statistic. A T-distribution table was used to get the critical values by using the decision risk of 2.5% and the degrees of freedom. The values were then plotted on a histogram and the null/alternative hypothesis was resolved.

In this project, I learned proper data gathering techniques and how to format the data on excel worksheets for calculation. Based on the data collected, the correct type of test to carry out was decided. The significance was then determined by identifying a hypothesis, calculating the test statistic and then making a decision based on the results and other influences. The results were then visualised by using DataDesk which helped determine the significance of the results.
