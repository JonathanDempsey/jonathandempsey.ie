LOCOMOTIVE

3D Modelling Using 3DMax

BACK TO MAIN

A locomotive train was created using 3DMax. Once the model was finished, a small animation was made to display the model in a showcase video.

Blueprints of locomotives were found and used to trace the general shape of the locomotive. The main body of the train consisted of many cylinders and tubes connected to one another while placed on a sort of 'stage' (The part that connects the wheels with the main body). A lot of the pieces used here required slight touches so modifiers such as 'Edit Poly' and 'Extrudes' were used to make the pieces connect as one. The details on top of the main body were made by using the line tool and lathe to create the complicated objects. The drivers pit was made by creating a large box and using edit poly to make it hollow. Then for the windows I put tubes placed into the hollow box and used edit poly to delete the blank spaces to be able to see in and out of the pit.

The video was made by making several iterations of the cameras movement viewing the model, then applied to after effects to put in opacity, edit timing and sound, and finally exported as a quicktime.mov to YouTube.

Simple materials were added to the model as glossy and solid colours. The camera tool was then used to create a panning clip of the model. The clip was moved into After Effects where final details were added before being exported to YouTube.
